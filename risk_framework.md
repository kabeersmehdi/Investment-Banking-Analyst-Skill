## RISK CATEGORIES & TRIGGERS

### Category 1: Financial Risk

| Risk | Severity Trigger | Mitigation Narrative |
|------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Revenue declining 2+ yrs | ⚠️ High | Document cause; show stabilization data |
| EBITDA margin shrinking | ⚠️ Medium | Identify root cause; show correction plan |
| Add-backs > 30% net income | ⚠️ High | Itemize and document with receipts |
| Tax returns only, no P&L | ⚠️ Medium | Reconstruct from bank statements; offer QoE |
| Working capital deficit | ⚠️ Medium | Normalize; explain seasonality |
| Deferred capital needs | ⚠️ High | Quantify; adjust purchase price or structure |

### Category 2: Customer Concentration Risk

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Single customer > 25% | ⚠️ High | Show contract, tenure, relationship depth |
| Single customer > 40% | 🚨 Deal-Risk | Earnout / seller note to align incentives |
| Top 3 > 60% | ⚠️ High | Diversification roadmap; relationship audit |
| No written contracts | ⚠️ Medium | Show historical retention rate (X yrs) |
| Customer declining / at risk | 🚨 Deal-Risk | Must disclose; may require price adjustment |

### Category 3: Key Person / Operational Risk

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Owner = primary salesperson | ⚠️ High | Transition plan; customer intros; shadow period |
| Owner = sole technical expert | ⚠️ High | Document processes; train backup |
| No management team | ⚠️ Medium | Identify key employees; retention bonuses |
| Single location, owner daily | ⚠️ Medium | Show remote management precedent |
| High employee turnover | ⚠️ Medium | Show comp vs. market; culture narrative |

### Category 4: Market / Industry Risk (Live-Sourced)

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Cyclical industry | ⚠️ Medium | Show through-cycle performance data |
| Commoditized market | ⚠️ Medium | Identify differentiation; customer loyalty data |
| Regulatory exposure | ⚠️ High | Document compliance history |
| Technology disruption | ⚠️ Medium | Show adaptation; tech investment |
| Labor market tightness | ⚠️ Medium | Show compensation structure; retention rate |
🌐 [Source live industry-specific risks here]

### Category 5: Structural / Legal Risk

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Lease < 18 months remaining | ⚠️ High | Get landlord LOI for renewal pre-LOI |
| Pending litigation | 🚨 Deal-Risk | Full disclosure; escrow holdback |
| IP not owned by entity | ⚠️ High | Clean up pre-sale; legal opinion |
| Environmental liability | 🚨 Deal-Killer | Phase 1 ESA required |
| Revenue recognition issues | ⚠️ High | CPA review; adjust SDE accordingly |

## RISK SEVERITY RATING SYSTEM

| Rating | Criteria | Deal Impact |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Low | Easily mitigated, minor financial impact | None — disclose briefly |
| Medium | Requires explanation; may affect multiple | Minor discount possible |
| High | Significant; buyer will flag in diligence | Narrative required; may affect price |
| Deal-Risk | Could kill deal or require restructuring | Major mitigation needed |
| Deal-Killer | Unresolvable without significant action | Fix before going to market |

## NARRATIVE SCRIPTS FOR COMMON RED FLAGS

### "The owner is the business"
*Narrative:*
"The current owner has been instrumental in building [COMPANY]'s
reputation over [X] years. However, the company's success is rooted
in the quality and reliability of its delivery — not in the owner
personally. [X] of the top [X] customer relationships are also
maintained by tenured team members. The seller is prepared to offer
a [X]-month transition period with formal customer introductions
and is open to a consulting arrangement during Year 1."

### "Revenue is concentrated"
*Narrative:*
"While [X] customers represent [X]% of revenue, these relationships
average [X] years in duration and are supported by [contracts /
master service agreements / long-term purchase orders]. Buyer
diligence will confirm the depth and stickiness of these
relationships. The Company has never experienced involuntary
customer attrition in its [X]-year history."

### "Financials are messy"
*Narrative:*
"As is common with founder-owned businesses of this size, financial
reporting has prioritized tax efficiency over GAAP presentation.
The broker has reconstructed trailing performance from source
documents, bank statements, and owner records. A Quality of Earnings
review is available post-LOI upon reasonable request. Add-backs
are fully documented and conservatively applied."

### "Revenue is declining"
*Narrative:*
"Revenue in [Year] was impacted by [specific factor — owner health /
COVID / lost contract / intentional pruning of low-margin work].
Core operations and customer relationships remain intact. TTM
revenue of $[X]M reflects [stabilization / recovery / strategic
refocus] and represents the true run-rate of the business going
forward. A pipeline of $[X]M in qualified opportunities supports
the growth case."

### "Owner works 60+ hours per week"
*Narrative:*
"The owner's involvement reflects their personal dedication to the
business rather than an operational requirement. Key processes are
documented and executable by the existing team. The seller has
agreed to a [X]-month full-time transition and will remain available
as a consultant thereafter. A replacement GM/operator earning
$[X]/year has been factored into the normalized SDE calculation."
